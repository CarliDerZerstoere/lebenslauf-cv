## Core Studies (24 ECTS)

### Principles of Secure Software Design (MTAT.03.307) — 6 ECTS | Grade: 2
**What I learned:** Systematic approach to designing security into software from the requirements phase onward, based on Prof. Matulevičius's textbook.
- Security requirements engineering: asset identification, risk analysis
- Threat modeling using STRIDE methodology
- Misuse cases and abuse cases
- Security patterns and model-driven security
- Secure system modelling with UML and SecBPMN
- **Tools:** UML, BPMN/SecBPMN, STRIDE, threat modeling tools

### Human Aspects of Cyber Security (ITC8210) — 6 ECTS | Grade: 5
**What I learned:** How human behavior, cognitive biases, and decision-making create cybersecurity vulnerabilities — and how to counter them.
- Social engineering attacks: phishing, pretexting, manipulation
- Cognitive biases that compromise security decisions
- Designing and evaluating security awareness training
- Cyber competency analysis and gap identification
- Planning social-engineering attacks and proposing defences
- Academic research evaluation in human-centered cybersecurity
- **Assessment:** 3,000-word essay + 10-minute oral presentation

- Cybercrime definitions, criminal liability, prosecution
- Digital evidence: acquisition, chain of custody, legal admissibility
- Data protection and privacy law (GDPR)
- Ethics of cybersecurity and ethical hacking boundaries
- International legal instruments (EU legislation, CoE Cybercrime Convention)
- Electronic communications law

### Cyber Security Technologies I (ITC8250) — 6 ECTS | Grade: 5

- Adversarial threat modeling: Cyber Kill Chain, threat-based defence
- Malware types, behavior, and rapid analysis techniques
- Exploits and vulnerability concepts
- Firewalls, IDS/IPS configuration
- Network forensics and disk forensics fundamentals

## Special Studies in Cyber Security (60 ECTS)

### Thesis Seminar (ITC8200) — 6 ECTS | Grade: A
**What I learned:** Scientific methodology and academic research skills for thesis preparation.
- Nature of science and scientific reasoning
- Defining research questions, conducting literature reviews
- Academic writing, presentation, and peer critique
- Thesis topic registration and supervisor matching

### Cryptography (ITC8240) — 6 ECTS | Grade: 0/1
**What I learned:** Rigorous theoretical and applied cryptography from classical ciphers to post-quantum algorithms.
- Mathematical foundations: number theory, modular arithmetic, abstract algebra
- Symmetric cryptography: stream ciphers, block ciphers (DES, AES), modes of operation
- Key exchange: Diffie-Hellman, man-in-the-middle attacks
- RSA cryptosystem: properties, security analysis, implementation vulnerabilities
- Digital signatures, hash functions, zero-knowledge proofs
- PKI: certificates, Estonian ID-Card, TLS
- Post-quantum cryptography: lattice-based systems (ML-KEM, ML-DSA)

### System Forensics (ITX8200) — 6 ECTS | Grade: 5
**What I learned:** Hands-on digital forensics — evidence acquisition, analysis, and chain of custody using open-source tools.
- Forensic evidence acquisition (disk imaging, memory dumping)
- Memory forensics and unstructured data analysis
- File system forensics: FAT, FAT32, NTFS, Ext4
- Data carving and recovery of deleted data
- Email, browser, and registry forensics
- USB device and smartphone forensics
- Timeline creation and event reconstruction
- Chain of evidence documentation
- **Tools:** Open-source forensic tools, FTK Imager, Volatility concepts
- **Textbook:** "Digital Forensics with Open Source Tools" (Altheide & Carvey)

- **Tools:** Sharemind MPC platform, SecreC programming language
